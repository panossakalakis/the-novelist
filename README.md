# The Novelist — Free Obsidian Vault

**The Novelist** is a free, opinionated Obsidian vault designed for novelists and long‑form fiction writers.  
It gives you a structured, colorful workspace to plan, write, and revise your novel—without fighting your tools.

- Worldbuilding hub with Canvas.
- Character & location systems.
- Chapter management workflow.
- Writing goals & progress tracking.
- Theme‑aware, minimal, and extensible.

## Features

### Worldbuilding canvas

A pre‑built **Canvas** acts as the control center of your novel:

- **Characters**
- **Locations**
- **Chapters**
- **Notes & ideas**
- **To‑do list**
- **Book details & cover**

Use it as a visual map to navigate your story, brainstorm, and keep the big picture in view.

### Chapter management

The vault includes a dedicated **Chapters** area:

- **Master chapter index** listing all chapters.
- **Individual chapter files** for focused drafting.
- Suggested workflow:
  - Draft in separate chapter files.
  - Optionally, copy the final text into an “All Chapters” file as a backup or for export.

### Templates for characters & locations

Two core templates are included:

- **Character template**
- **Location template**

They are wired to work with the **Templater** plugin so you can:

- Create new characters/locations with consistent structure.
- Keep worldbuilding organized and searchable.
- Quickly expand your cast and settings as you write.

### Graph‑driven story insights

Once you start linking:

- Characters ↔ Chapters  
- Locations ↔ Chapters  
- Characters ↔ Locations  

…the **Graph View** becomes a story tool:

- Spot underused characters or locations.
- See which POVs dominate.
- Discover missing connections and potential subplots.

### Multitasking layout

The vault is designed to work well with Obsidian’s split views:

- Write a chapter in one pane.
- Keep character sheets open in another window.
- Pin locations or notes on the side.
- Rearrange as needed for your personal workflow.

## Requirements

### Theme

The vault is built around the **AnuPpuccin** theme (with a few optional tweaks).  
You can still switch themes, but the default look is optimized for AnuPpuccin.

### Recommended community plugins

These plugins power most of the “smart” behavior in the vault:

| Plugin              | Role                                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Editing Toolbar** | Adds a visual formatting toolbar for Markdown editing.     |
| **Iconize**         | Adds icons to files, folders, and UI elements.             |
| **Novel Word Count**| Shows word/page counts next to files and folders.         |
| **Style Settings**  | Lets you customize theme colors and typography.           |
| **Templater**       | Enables dynamic templates (characters, locations, etc.)  |
| **Writing Goals**   | Track writing goals per chapter or project.               |
| **Kanban**          | Trello‑style boards for tasks, arcs, or chapter planning. |

Core Obsidian plugins used include:

- **Canvas**
- **Daily Notes**
- **Graph View**
- **Templates**
- **Word Count**
- …and others you can enable/disable as you like.

## Installation

### 1. Download the vault

- Download the files from here (GitHub) or go to the Gumroad page:  
  **https://sakalakis.gumroad.com/l/the-novelist-free-obsidian-vault**
- Download the `.zip` file.

### 2. Extract the archive

- **Windows:** Right‑click → **Extract Here**
- **macOS:** Double‑click the `.zip`
- **Linux:** Right‑click → **Extract Here**

You should now have a folder named something like `The Novelist`.

### 3. Open in Obsidian

1. Open **Obsidian**
2. Click your vault name (bottom‑left) → **Manage vaults…**
3. Under **Open folder as vault**, click **Open**.
4. Select the extracted **The Novelist** folder.
5. When prompted, enable **Community plugins** and trust the vault.


## Customization

You’re encouraged to tweak the vault to match your style.

### Style & colors

1. Enable the **Style Settings** plugin.
2. Go to **Settings → Style Settings**.
3. Adjust:
   - Colors
   - Fonts
   - Spacing
   - UI density

### Structure & plugins

- Remove folders you don’t use.
- Add your own templates.
- Disable plugins you don’t need.
- Swap the theme if you prefer a different look.

The vault is a starting point.
